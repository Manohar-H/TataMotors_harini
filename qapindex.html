WELCOME TO TATA MOTORS

About Tata Motors

On September 1st 1945, Tata Motors was founded and it has evolved from its early days as a locomotive manufacturer under the name TELCO but soon expanded into an automobile manufacturing powerhouse of innovation, producing cars, buses, trucks, and defense vehicles. Today, the company is at the forefront of sustainability and e-mobility, with a strong focus on cutting-edge electric vehicles (EVs) and advanced engineering.


Here's a quote from Natarajan Chandrasekaran, Chairman of Tata Motors, which sums up the company's vision:

Our goal is to drive sustainable mobility by producing world-class cars that offer safety, comfort,and performance while reducing our environmental footprint.

Global Presence and Reach

Headquartered in Mumbai, Tata Motors operates in over 125 countries across the globe. With manufacturing facilities spread across India, Europe, the UK, South Korea, and South America, the company ensures that it delivers innovative and high-performing vehicles worldwide. They also have a robust workforce dedicated to building vehicles that enhance the driving experience and meet the needs of customers across all segments.

A Legacy of Social Responsibility

At Tata Motors, sustainability extends beyond the vehicles it produces. The company has embedded CSR into its operations, actively working on initiatives related to environmental conservation, education, healthcare, and community development. The company has a dedicated focus on reducing carbon emissions, promoting energy efficiency in its plants, and adopting circular economy principles.

Innovations in Design and Technology

Tata Motors has a long-standing commitment to innovation. It made a major breakthrough in India's passenger vehicle market with the launch of the Tata Indica in 1998, the country's first indigenously developed car. Since then, the company has expanded its portfolio to include iconic models such as the Nexon and Harrier, featuring bold designs and safety-first engineering

One of Tata's biggest successes in recent years is the Nexon, which became India's first vehicle to earn a 5-star safety rating from Global NCAP (New Car Assessment Program). This SUV, along with models like the Tiago and Altroz, showcases Tata Motors' commitment to quality and customer satisfaction, balancing cutting-edge technology with affordability

Sustainability and Electric Mobility

Tata Motors has taken bold steps toward sustainability with the development of its electric vehicle (EV) lineup, which includes models such as the Nexon EV and Tigor EV. These vehicles embody Tata’s vision for cleaner, greener mobility, offering customers a zero-emission alternative to conventional fuel cars. By 2030, Tata Motors aims for EVs to contribute up to 50% of its total sales.

To support the transition to electric mobility, Tata Motors has introduced Ziptron technology, which powers its EVs and promises superior performance, long battery life, and quick charging capabilities. This marks a significant step in Tata Motors’ mission to drive the future of transportation, not only in India but globally.

The Road Ahead

With a solid foundation of over seven decades in the automotive industry, Tata Motors continues to push boundaries. The company's recent partnerships, acquisitions, and investments in electric mobility solutions demonstrate its dedication to evolving alongside market demands. Tata Motors is well-positioned to lead the global charge toward safer, cleaner, and more efficient vehicles.
